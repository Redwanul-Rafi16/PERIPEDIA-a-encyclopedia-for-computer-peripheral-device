Printer

What is a Printer?

A printer is an output device that produces a hard copy (permanent human-readable text and/or graphics) of documents stored electronically on a computer. It prints information onto physical media such as paper or transparencies. Printers may be connected directly to a computer or through a network.

Basic Technologies of Printers

Printer technologies are divided into two main categories:

1. Impact Printers

Impact printers create images by physically striking the paper through an ink ribbon.

Types of Impact Printers:
  • Dot Matrix Printer
  • Character Printer

2. Non-Impact Printers

Non-impact printers create images without touching the paper directly.

Types of Non-Impact Printers:
  • Inkjet Printer
  • Laser Printer
  • Solid Ink Printer

Working Principle

The printing process and mechanical operation vary significantly based on the underlying technology. Below are the detailed working procedures for the three primary printer types:

Inkjet Printer

The inkjet printer printing process works by spraying tiny droplets of ink onto paper to create text and images.

fig: components of inkjet printer
Working Procedure:
  1. Print Command: The computer sends the document data to the printer driver, which converts it into a format the printer can understand.
  2. Data Transfer: The processed data is sent from the computer to the printer and stored in the printer's memory (buffer).
  3. Paper Feeding: The paper feed motor activates rollers that pull a sheet of paper into the printer.
  4. Printing: The print head moves back and forth across the paper, spraying tiny droplets of ink through its nozzles to form text and images.
  5. Color Formation: The printer combines CMYK inks (Cyan, Magenta, Yellow, and Black) in precise amounts to produce different colors.
  6. Paper Advancement: After each pass of the print head, the paper moves forward slightly, and the process repeats until the entire page is printed.

Completion: Once printing is finished, the print head is parked and the printed page is pushed into the output tray.

Laser Printer

A laser printer uses static electricity, a laser beam, toner powder, and heat to print text and images on paper.

fig: the basic components of a laser printer
Working Procedure:
  1. Charging the Drum: The photoconductive drum is given a uniform electrical charge.
  2. Writing the Image: A laser beam scans the drum and creates an electrostatic image by discharging selected areas corresponding to the text or graphics to be printed.
  3. Applying Toner: Positively charged toner powder sticks to the discharged areas on the drum, forming the image.
  4. Transferring to Paper: The paper is given an electrical charge and passes under the drum. The toner is attracted from the drum onto the paper.
  5. Fusing: The paper passes through heated rollers called the fuser, which melt the toner and permanently bond it to the paper.
  6. Cleaning and Recharging: The drum is cleaned and recharged, making it ready for the next print cycle.

Dot Matrix Printer

A Dot Matrix Printer is an impact printer that prints by striking an ink-soaked ribbon against paper using tiny pins.

fig: components of a dot matrix printer
Working Procedure:
  1. Data Reception: The printer receives text or graphics data from the computer.
  2. Print Head Movement: The print head moves back and forth across the paper.
  3. Pin Activation: Small metal pins (wires) in the print head are activated by electromagnets.
  4. Impact on Ribbon: The pins strike an ink-soaked ribbon, which transfers ink onto the paper.
  5. Character Formation: Multiple dots are printed close together to form letters, numbers, and graphics.
  6. Paper Advancement: After printing a line, the paper moves up, and the process repeats until the page is completed.

Which one is better for buying?

Select the technology that matches your usage requirements and printing budget:

1. For home / student use: Inkjet Printer

Good for color printing, assignments, photos. However, ink cost is higher over time due to frequent cartridge replacements.

2. For office / high-volume printing: Laser Printer (Best overall choice)

Fast, clean, and cheap per page. It is the ideal choice for documents, reports, and large-scale bulk printing operations.

3. For billing, shops, banks: Dot Matrix Printer

Works with carbon copies to print multiple duplicate copies simultaneously. Extremely durable and cost-effective, though outdated for general document layout work.

Real-Life Applications

How Printers are utilized across primary organizational sectors:

Education Sector

Used for printing syllabus files, assignments, student report submissions, and study materials.

Business Environment

Used for printouts of invoices, business reports, marketing proposals, employee handbooks, and contracts.

Healthcare Sector

Used for printing physical patient logs, laboratory results, medical receipts, and drug prescriptions.

Banking Sector

Generates paper records of loans, credit audits, ledger logs, and paper balance statements.

Home Usage

Used for printing recipes, flight tickets, children's worksheets, and personal digital photos.

Graphic Design & Multimedia

Invaluable for drawing curves, positioning vectors, dragging timelines in video editors, and precision painting.
